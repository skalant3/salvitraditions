The origins

Tonacatepeque, El Salvador

Tonacatepeque is just 15 kilometers northeast of San Salvador. Tonacatepeque is a Nahuatl word, an indigenous language historically spoken in the region. The name Tonacatepeque means "Hill of Abundance" or "Place of Abundance." Tonacatepeque stands a historic Church, a testament to the town's deep-rooted traditions and architectural beauty. Stroll along cobblestone streets to discover quaint cafes, where you can savor local flavors and immerse yourself in the laid-back atmosphere.

What is La Calabiuza?

Traditional dish to celebrate La Calabiuza Spooky tradition prior the Day of the Dead in El Salvador!

La Calabiuza is the name of a tradition celebrated the day before the Day of the Dead (Día de los Difuntos) in El Salvador. It is called Calabiuza because the community prepares the traditional dish called "Ayote en Miel" (Squash in Honey). It is a tradition for the municipality of Tonacatepeque to prepare and share (for free) this culinary delight to its residents and visitors. The name "La Calabiuza" is derived from the Spanish word "Calabaza" (pumpkin) and the colloquial Salvadoran word for Squash is Ayote, from which this dish is prepared.

Spooky tradition caught in camera: Experience the Annual Calabiuza Festivity in these videos!

The Calabiuza Festival in Tonacatepeque, El Salvador, happens on November 1 before The Day of the Dead ("Día de los Difuntos"). It’s about Salvadoran stories like Cipitio and Siguanaba. This year (2023), rain delayed it to November 4. People dress up, pull spooky carts, and ask for squash (Ayote). Everyone, locals and tourists, loves watching this unique celebration.
